A small security patch on top of 1.5.3. While Guard was actively holding you — a pause, a disconnect, or a strict lock in effect — you could still turn the monitor off from the HUD (the toggle or the ✕) and stop the whole thing. That was a one-tap escape from the very enforcement that is meant to be inescapable. It is now closed.

### Fixed: you can't switch Meridian off mid-enforcement

While any Guard enforcement is active — a pause, a disconnect, or a strict lock — turning Meridian's monitoring off (the HUD toggle or the ✕ button) is now refused, with a short note telling you what is holding it. The moment the pause or lock releases, it works normally again.

Everything else is unchanged:

- When **nothing** is being enforced, you can turn Meridian off exactly as before — the toggle and the ✕ both still work. Closing the app is never taken away from you.
- **Minimize** the HUD any time — monitoring keeps running in the background.
- **Closing or reducing your open positions is never blocked**, enforcement or not.

### Who should upgrade

Everyone on 1.5.x — especially anyone who relies on Strict Lock, since this closes the one-tap bypass. Your profile, Guard rules, journal, and history are preserved automatically.

### How to upgrade

1. In NinjaTrader 8: **Tools > Remove NinjaScript Assembly**, select **MeridianPSI**, and click **Remove** (your settings and data are kept).
2. **Tools > Import > NinjaScript Add-On**, and select `MeridianPSI_V1.5.4.zip`.
3. Let NinjaTrader compile and restart if prompted. Your profile, Guard rules, journal, and history carry over automatically.
