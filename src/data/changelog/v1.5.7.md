If you trade more than one funded account, this release changes how your daily-loss stop works — for the better. It also carries a few fixes that came straight from a live multi-account trader, and a plain-language pass so the app reads like English, not internals. **As always, closing or reducing a position is never blocked — Guard only ever stops you opening new risk.**

### New: your daily-loss stop is now per account

If you set a **"session loss reaches $X"** Guard rule and you're monitoring more than one account, that stop now watches **each account's own realised loss**. When one account hits its limit, Guard stops **that** account — it blocks new entries on it, and closes it if you ticked "also close my open positions" — while your other accounts keep trading untouched.

Before, the daily-loss rule added every account into one number. A winning account could hide a losing one, so the stop might not fire while a single account quietly blew its own limit — and when it did fire, it acted across all your accounts. Now each funded account is guarded on its own line, which is what a multi-account funded trader actually needs.

A few honest specifics:

- **Single account? Nothing changes.** With one monitored account the behaviour is exactly as before — the per-account path only engages with two or more accounts.
- This applies to a plain **"session loss reaches $X"** rule. A rule that *combines* session loss with other conditions stays whole-session, as it was — it is never silently changed.
- Your **PSI and every other Guard rule are unchanged.** Only the dollar daily-loss stop became per-account.
- The Accounts card now also shows each account's own realised P&L and how far it is from its daily stop.

### Fixed

- **No more false "no stop" warning** when an ATM strategy places its entry and protective stop a beat apart — the alert now waits out that normal timing race instead of crying wolf.
- **A live data backfill no longer resets your session.** On some setups, the historical fills that stream in on connect or reconnect could wipe your live session's stats and PSI. That reset now only happens where it belongs (replaying in Playback), never when live data is simply catching up.
- **Quieter logs** — the routine, self-healing "ghost position" notes during a backfill are recorded calmly instead of as alarms.

### Clearer wording

A pass over the app's on-screen text to replace leaked internal shorthand with plain English — so what Meridian tells you reads like a sentence, not a debug code. Your trading terms and numbers are untouched; only the internal jargon changed.
