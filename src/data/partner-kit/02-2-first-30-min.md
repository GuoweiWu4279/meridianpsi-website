The fastest way to understand Meridian is to use it in a Market Replay session — you can run through a real trading session at any speed without risking capital, and Meridian will monitor your behavior exactly as it does live.

---

**Minutes 1–5: Get oriented**

Open the Meridian Hub (Tools → Meridian → Open Hub). You'll see:

- **PSI History** — a chart of your stability score across past sessions (empty for now)
- **Session Summary** — stats from the most recent session
- **Profile** — your adaptive baseline settings and signal weights
- **Guard** (if you're on the Guard tier) — intervention rules configuration

Spend a minute on each tab. Don't configure anything yet — just get a feel for the layout.

---

**Minutes 5–20: Run a Market Replay session**

1. In NT8: **Tools → Historical Data** — pick a recent date with active trading on the instrument you use
2. Start the replay at roughly 1–2x speed
3. Trade it as you normally would — the goal is to behave naturally, not to perform well
4. Watch the PSI HUD while you trade. Notice which signals move when you make specific decisions

What to look for:
- Does the PSI drop after a losing trade? How quickly does it recover?
- Which of the seven dimensions moves first when you feel frustrated or impatient?
- Does the score look different when you're trading well vs. when you're forcing trades?

---

**Minutes 20–30: Review the session**

After stopping the replay, go back to the Hub and look at the Session Summary. You'll see:
- Your PSI timeline across the session
- Which dimensions spiked and when
- Any Guard triggers (if configured)

The first session baseline is thin — Meridian doesn't have much history to compare against yet. After 5–10 sessions, the signals become meaningfully more precise.

---

**One thing to notice:** Meridian Replay sessions are tracked separately from live sessions and do not affect your live baseline. You can run as many replay sessions as you want during your trial without skewing the data that matters.
