**The rule in one sentence:** Any time you earn money or receive free product for promoting something, you must disclose it. With Meridian, you receive a free Guard license and earn 30% recurring commission — both qualify.

**What the FTC requires:** The disclosure must be *clear and conspicuous* — meaning it appears before the affiliate link, not buried at the end of a long description, and not in a collapsed "more" section.

**What it does not require:** Exact wording. The disclosures above are designed to be accurate and natural-sounding, not robotic legal copy. You can adapt them to your voice as long as the substance is clear: you earn a commission.

**The one thing you cannot do:** Present a sponsored recommendation as an organic, unpaid opinion. Everything else — tone, phrasing, placement — is flexible.

If you're unsure whether a specific piece of content requires a disclosure, the safe answer is: if you're promoting Meridian and you have an affiliate arrangement, disclose it.
